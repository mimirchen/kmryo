JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions  ·  2026-07-13
A Rigshospitalet-led international position statement from 30+ experts proposes the TAVR CODE framework (Coaxiality, Orientation, Depth, Expansion) of four fluoroscopic parameters to standardize intraprocedural TAVR assessment, and details optimization for younger, longer-life-expectancy patients — pre/post-dilatation, commissural alignment, IVUS expansion checks and AI-robotic delivery.

TAVI JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions · 2026-07-13
Hospital Volume and Failure to Rescue Post-TAVR: Insights From the STS/ACC TVT Registry
A 487,159-patient STS/ACC TVT registry cohort (808 sites, 2017–2023): low-volume centers had higher 30-day mortality (adj OR 1.12) and major complications (adj OR 1.19) than high-volume, but failure-to-rescue (11.0% overall) did not differ by volume — implying the volume-mortality link is driven by complication rates, not rescue ability.
OTHER JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions · 2026-07-14
SCAI/ASE/HRS/SIR/SVS Expert Consensus Statement on Enhanced Radiation Protection
A SCAI-led, multi-society expert consensus urges mandatory adoption of enhanced radiation-protection devices to meet ALARA, addressing cath-lab occupational risks (cancer, cataract, reproductive harm, lead-apron orthopedic injury), assigning responsibility to government, manufacturers and institutions and calling for updated thresholds — directly relevant to fluoroscopy-heavy structural teams.
TAVI JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions · 2026-07-13
Aortic Valve Hemodynamic Physiology: New Insights to Improve Patient Selection
A review of the physiologic basis for hemodynamic assessment in aortic stenosis, exploring new invasive indices (including the stress aortic valve index, SAVI) to improve TAVR patient selection in difficult low-flow/low-gradient cases — extending FFR-style physiology into structural heart.
OTHER MedTech Dive / FDA · 2026-07-14
Medtronic recalls Harmony heart valve delivery catheter for risk of tip detaching (FDA Class I)
The FDA classified Medtronic's recall of the Harmony transcatheter pulmonary valve delivery catheter as Class I (most serious): the distal tip can detach during implantation and require additional surgery to retrieve; 1,800+ devices worldwide (no serious injuries reported as of May 14). The valve is unaffected and implanted patients need no action.
TAVI EuroIntervention · 2026-07-17
Glucocorticoids to reduce permanent pacemaker implantation after TAVI: the GLUCO-TAVI randomised trial
The GLUCO-TAVI randomized trial (EuroIntervention 2026;22:545-554) enrolled 100 severe aortic stenosis patients undergoing TAVI, randomizing them to peri-procedural IV methylprednisolone plus 5 days of oral prednisone versus standard care. Glucocorticoids proved feasible and safe, with a numerical but not statistically significant reduction in 1-month permanent pacemaker implantation — a signal that needs confirmation in a larger trial.
IMAGING EuroIntervention · 2026-07-17
Prognostic value of early haemodynamic valve deterioration after TAVI
An international multicentre registry (16 centres, published in EuroIntervention; presented as a EuroPCR 2026 Hotline by Trimaille and colleagues) defined early hemodynamic valve deterioration (HVD) as a ≥10 mmHg rise in mean transaortic gradient within 3 months post-TAVI versus discharge echo. Over 8 years of follow-up, early HVD was linked to lower long-term valve-related clinical efficacy and a higher stroke rate, with lack of discharge anticoagulation an independent predictor — underscoring the value of early post-TAVI echo surveillance for risk stratification.
TRICUSPID EHJ · 2026-07-14
Acute haemodynamic instability after transcatheter tricuspid valve replacement: insights from pressure-volume loops
A pressure-volume-loop study of the mechanism of acute hemodynamic instability after transcatheter tricuspid valve replacement, showing that abruptly abolishing TR raises RV afterload and can precipitate RV decompensation — a physiologic basis for peri-procedural TTVR management.
TAVI JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions · 2026-07-13
Understanding the Volume-Outcome Curve in Contemporary TAVR: Complications or Failure to Rescue?
An editorial on the TVT registry volume analysis unpacks the mechanism — higher mortality at low-volume centers stems from more complications rather than worse rescue — and its implications for TAVR center accreditation, regionalization and quality improvement.
TAVI JACC: Cardiovascular Interventions · 2026-07-13
Iron Deficiency Criteria for Patients Undergoing Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement
An Erasmus MC (Rotterdam) cardiology-geriatrics research letter evaluates how different iron-deficiency criteria apply and differ in detection in a TAVR population; iron deficiency is a common, correctable comorbidity tied to recovery and quality of life, so a unified definition aids peri-procedural optimization.
IMAGING JACC: Case Reports · 2026-07-15
Echocardiographer-Led Combined Transjugular ICE and TEE for Tricuspid Transcatheter Edge-to-Edge Repair
A case of echocardiographer-led combined transjugular ICE and TEE guidance for tricuspid TEER, solving the common problem of poor TEE windows in tricuspid procedures — highlighting ICE's growing role and a new imaging-team collaboration model.
MITRAL TCTMD · 2026-07-17
REPAIR Suggests Favorable M-TEER Results Possible in Small Mitral Valves
The REPAIR registry — 1,189 patients with severe mitral regurgitation treated with Pascal M-TEER across 14 German centers (2019–2024) — presented new analyses at New York Valves 2026. With 28% of patients having a baseline mitral valve orifice area under 4 cm², a small orifice area did not meaningfully affect procedural success or clinical benefit, offering real-world reassurance for M-TEER in this anatomically challenging subgroup.
